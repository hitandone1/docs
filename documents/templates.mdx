Templates let you create fully pre-structured pages with a single click, eliminating the setup time that slows down recurring work. Whether you need a meeting agenda, a product spec, or a sprint plan, Jetie's template gallery puts a polished starting point one click away — and you can build your own templates from any page you've already created.

## Built-In Template Gallery

Jetie ships with a curated set of templates covering the most common document types. Open the gallery by creating a new page and clicking **Use template**.

Additional built-in templates include **Weekly Review**, **Knowledge Base Article**, **Bug Report**, and **Sprint Planning**. Browse the full gallery inside the app for previews of each.

## Applying a Template

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a new page">
    Click **+ New Page** at the bottom of the sidebar, or press `Cmd/Ctrl + N`. A blank untitled page opens in the editor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the template picker">
    Click **Use template** in the centre of the empty page, or select it from the **`...`** page menu at the top right.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse the gallery">
    Browse by category (Work, Personal, Engineering, HR, and more) or type a keyword in the search box to filter results.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview and select">
    Click any template thumbnail to see a full preview. When you're ready, click **Use this template** to apply it to the current page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Creating a Custom Template

Turn any page you've built into a reusable template so your team can reproduce it instantly.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the page menu">
    Navigate to the page you want to save as a template. Click the **`...`** icon in the top-right corner of the editor to open the page menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save as template">
    Select **Save as template** from the menu. A dialog appears asking you to confirm and name the template.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name your template">
    Enter a clear, descriptive name — for example, *"Q3 Sprint Planning"* or *"Client Onboarding Checklist"* — then click **Save**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find it under My Templates">
    Your new template appears under the **My Templates** section in the gallery. Apply it to any new page exactly as you would a built-in template.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Include a short description in the first paragraph of your page before saving it as a template. That text appears as the preview description in the gallery, helping teammates pick the right template at a glance.
</Tip>

## Sharing Templates with Your Workspace

Workspace **Admins** and **Owners** can publish templates so every member can access them in a shared **Workspace Templates** section of the gallery.

1. Open a custom template from **My Templates**.
2. Click **`...`** → **Share with workspace**.
3. Confirm the action in the dialog.

To unpublish a shared template, follow the same steps and choose **Remove from workspace**.

<Note>
  Only Admins and Owners can share or remove workspace-level templates. Members can apply shared templates but cannot modify or delete them.
</Note>

## Template Variables

Templates support placeholders that auto-fill the moment you apply a template to a new page. Use double curly braces in your template's content:

| Variable | Fills With |
|---|---|
| `{{date}}` | Today's date in your workspace's date format |
| `{{owner}}` | The name of the user who applied the template |
| `{{workspace}}` | Your workspace name |
| `{{page_title}}` | The title you give the new page |

You can add any of these placeholders while editing your custom template. When a teammate applies the template, Jetie replaces every placeholder automatically — no manual editing required.

## Duplicating a Template to Modify It

If you want to adapt an existing template without changing the original, duplicate it first:

1. In the template gallery, hover over the template card.
2. Click the **`...`** overflow menu that appears on the card.
3. Select **Duplicate**.
4. The copy appears in **My Templates** with the name *"Copy of [original name]"* — rename and edit it freely.
