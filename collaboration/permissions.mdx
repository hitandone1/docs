Jetie uses a two-tier permission system. Workspace roles define what a person can do across your entire workspace, while page-level overrides let you grant or restrict access on a per-page basis. Together, they give you precise control over who sees and edits what.

## Workspace Roles

Every workspace member is assigned one of four roles. These roles set a baseline level of access that applies across all pages unless a page-level override is in place.

| Action | Owner | Admin | Member | Guest |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| Edit pages | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ |
| Delete pages | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ own pages | ❌ |
| Invite members | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ | ❌ |
| Manage billing | ✅ | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ |
| Delete workspace | ✅ | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ |
| Manage workspace settings | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ | ❌ |
| Access restricted pages | ✅ | ✅ | Only if added | Only if added |

### Role Descriptions

- **Owner** — Has unrestricted access to every feature, including billing management and permanent workspace deletion. There can only be one Owner per workspace, but ownership can be transferred to another Admin.
- **Admin** — Can manage members, adjust workspace settings, and access all pages. Admins cannot delete the workspace or manage billing.
- **Member** — The standard role for teammates. Members can create, edit, and delete their own pages and collaborate on any page they have access to. They cannot manage members or billing.
- **Guest** — Can only access pages explicitly shared with them. Guests are read-only by default, though page-level overrides can grant a guest comment or edit access on specific pages.

<Note>
  On the **Free plan**, you can add a maximum of **5 guests** to your workspace. Upgrade to **Pro** for up to 20 guests, or **Business** for unlimited guests. Guests do not consume a paid member seat.
</Note>

## Changing a Member's Role

1. Open **Settings** from the workspace menu in the top-left corner.
2. Click **Members** in the sidebar.
3. Find the member whose role you want to change.
4. Click the **role dropdown** next to their name.
5. Select the new role. The change takes effect immediately — no confirmation email is sent to the member.

<Warning>
  Downgrading an Admin to Member removes their ability to invite new members and change workspace settings. Downgrading the Owner requires first transferring ownership to another Admin.
</Warning>

## Page-Level Permission Overrides

Workspace roles set the default, but you can override permissions for any individual on a specific page:

1. Open the page and click **…** (more options) → **Share**.
2. Under **People with access**, find the person or invite them by email.
3. Click the dropdown next to their name and select **Can view**, **Can comment**, or **Can edit**.

Page-level overrides work in both directions:

- **Grant more access**: Give a Guest edit permissions on a single page without promoting them to Member across the whole workspace.
- **Restrict access**: Prevent a Member from editing a sensitive page by setting them to **Can view** for that specific page.

## Permission Inheritance

Sub-pages inherit their parent page's permission settings by default. For example, if you share a parent page with a guest and set them to **Can comment**, every sub-page under it automatically grants that guest the same access.

To override inheritance on a sub-page:

1. Open the sub-page and go to **…** → **Share**.
2. Disable **Inherit parent permissions**.
3. Set the permissions for this page independently.

<Note>
  When you move a page to a new parent, the page adopts the new parent's inherited permissions. Review the page's share settings after any move to ensure access is still correct.
</Note>

## Restricting a Page

By default, all Members of a workspace can discover and view pages that are not explicitly restricted. To hide a page from Members not specifically invited:

1. Open the page and click **…** → **Restrict**.
2. Select **Only people with access**.
3. The page disappears from the workspace sidebar and search results for anyone not listed under **People with access**.

Admins and Owners can always see restricted pages, regardless of the page-level settings.

<Tip>
  Use restrictions for pages containing HR data, financial projections, or board materials. This way, the content lives in Jetie alongside your other work without being visible to the whole team.
</Tip>
