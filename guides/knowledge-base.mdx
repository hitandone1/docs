A well-organized knowledge base means your team spends less time asking repetitive questions and more time doing focused work. Jetie lets you build one without any special configuration — using the same pages, databases, and AI tools you already use for project work. This guide walks you through setting up a KB from scratch and keeping it current over time.

<Tip>
  Once your knowledge base is live, team members can ask questions directly in AI Chat — for example, *"What's our parental leave policy?"* or *"How do I set up a local dev environment?"* — and get answers drawn from across your KB pages without needing to navigate manually.
</Tip>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a top-level Knowledge Base page">
    Click **+ New Page** in the sidebar and name it **Knowledge Base**. Add a short description at the top of the page explaining what the KB covers and who maintains it. Then right-click the page in the sidebar and select **Pin to sidebar** — this ensures every workspace member can find the KB immediately, regardless of how the page hierarchy is organized.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create category sub-pages">
    Inside the Knowledge Base page, add a sub-page for each major topic area your team needs. Common categories include:

    - **Engineering** — architecture decisions, coding standards, runbooks
    - **Product** — roadmap context, feature specs, design principles
    - **HR & People** — policies, benefits, onboarding materials
    - **Onboarding** — new hire checklist, tool access guide, team norms
    - **Operations** — vendor contacts, process documentation, compliance

    Start with the categories that get the most questions, then expand as content grows. Each category page acts as a landing page for its section, so add a brief description at the top of each one.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Use the Knowledge Base Article template for each article">
    Inside a category page, click **+ Add sub-page**, then type `/template` and select **Knowledge Base Article**. This template includes:

    - **Title** — descriptive and searchable
    - **Owner** — the person responsible for keeping the article accurate
    - **Last Updated** — a date field updated each time the article is edited
    - **Summary** — a one-paragraph TL;DR at the top
    - **Content** — the main body with headings, lists, and any embedded assets

    Using a consistent template across all articles makes the KB predictable and easier to maintain.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add a database index page">
    Create a sub-page called **Article Index** directly inside the top-level Knowledge Base page. Add a **Database** block in **Table** view. Link it to your KB articles by using the **Relation** property to connect to pages across your workspace. Add filter and sort controls for **Category**, **Owner**, and **Tags** so teammates can find relevant articles without browsing the full hierarchy.

    <Note>
      The Article Index is especially useful for larger teams. When the KB grows to dozens of articles, the index becomes the fastest way to locate specific content.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enable AI search across the knowledge base">
    Jetie's AI Chat automatically indexes all pages in your workspace. To make the KB queryable:

    1. Go to **Settings → AI → Context sources**
    2. Confirm that your Knowledge Base pages are included in the workspace context
    3. Encourage teammates to ask questions using AI Chat before searching manually

    AI Chat can synthesize answers from multiple articles at once — for example, combining information from three different HR policy pages into a single, coherent response.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set permissions for the knowledge base">
    Open the Knowledge Base page, click **Share**, and configure access:

    - Set all **workspace members** to **Can view** by default
    - Assign the **Can edit** role only to designated editors or team leads for each category
    - Set **Admins** to **Full access** so they can restructure the KB as needed
    - Leave the **Allow guests to view** toggle off unless you intend to share KB content externally

    Apply the same permissions to all sub-pages by clicking **Apply to sub-pages** in the sharing dialog.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Keeping Your Knowledge Base Fresh

A knowledge base only stays useful if the content is accurate. Use these practices to prevent articles from going stale.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Assign an owner to every article" icon="user">
    Every KB article should have exactly one owner — the person who owns that domain and is responsible for keeping the article accurate. Set this in the **Owner** property on the Knowledge Base Article template.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Flag stale content" icon="clock">
    Use the **Last Updated** property on each article and filter the Article Index to surface anything not edited in the past 90 days. Reach out to the article owner to schedule a review.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Set quarterly review tasks" icon="calendar">
    Create a recurring task in your workspace calendar each quarter: *"Review KB articles for staleness."* Assign it to the KB owner. Link the task to the Article Index so the reviewer has one-click access to every article.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Importing Existing Documentation

If your team already has documentation in other formats, you don't need to recreate it from scratch.

<Accordion title="Import PDFs or Word documents">
  Go to **Settings → Import** and select **PDF** or **Word Document**. Upload the file, and Jetie converts it to a Jetie page with editable blocks. After import, open the page and apply the Knowledge Base Article template structure manually — add the Owner, Last Updated, and Summary fields at the top, then organize the body content with proper headings.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Import from Notion or Confluence">
  Go to **Settings → Import**, select **Notion** or **Confluence**, and follow the OAuth flow. Jetie maps your existing page hierarchy, blocks, and databases automatically. After the import, move the relevant pages into the appropriate KB category sub-pages and apply the Knowledge Base Article template to each one.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Paste content from Google Docs or other sources">
  Copy text from Google Docs, Microsoft Word, or any other source and paste it directly into a Jetie page. Jetie preserves headings, bold, italic, bullet lists, and tables. After pasting, clean up the formatting and add the KB article properties at the top of the page.
</Accordion>
