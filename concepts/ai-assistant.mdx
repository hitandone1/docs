Jetie AI is not a chatbot you open in a separate tab — it is embedded directly in your workspace, aware of the pages you are writing, the tasks on your plate, and the documents your team has shared. You can ask it a question and get an answer grounded in your actual content, or hand it a goal and watch it propose a step-by-step plan. Because it is semi-autonomous, it always shows you what it intends to do before changing anything, keeping you firmly in control.

## Two Modes

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Chat Mode">
    **Chat mode** is a conversational Q&A interface anchored to your workspace content. Ask Jetie AI anything about your pages, tasks, or documents and it will answer using the information it finds — citing the source page so you can verify.

    **What Chat mode is great for:**
    - "What did we decide about the pricing model in the Q2 planning doc?"
    - "Summarize all open tasks assigned to me this week."
    - "Find every page that mentions the API rate limit."
    - "Draft a status update for the mobile launch project."

    Chat mode reads the current page you have open, any pages linked from it via `@mentions`, and your task backlog. It does not modify any content — it only reads and responds.

    **How to open Chat mode:**
    1. Click the **AI** button in the top-right toolbar, or press `Cmd/Ctrl + J`.
    2. Type your question in the chat input and press `Enter`.
    3. Jetie AI responds inline. Click any cited page link to jump directly to the source.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Autopilot Mode">
    **Autopilot mode** is a semi-autonomous agent that decomposes a high-level goal into concrete tasks and executes multi-step actions across your workspace — with your approval at each stage.

    **What Autopilot mode is great for:**
    - "Break down the website redesign project into tasks and add them to my Sprint board."
    - "Create a project page for the new API integration with a roadmap, a decision log, and a task database."
    - "Find all incomplete tasks past their due date and move them to next week."

    **How Autopilot mode works:**
    1. Press `Cmd/Ctrl + J` and click **Autopilot** at the top of the AI panel.
    2. Describe your goal in plain language.
    3. Jetie AI shows you a **proposed action plan** — a list of every step it intends to take.
    4. Review the plan. Edit, remove, or reorder steps as needed.
    5. Click **Run** to execute. Jetie AI works through each step and reports its progress in real time.
    6. At any point, click **Pause** to stop and review before continuing.

    <Warning>
      Autopilot mode never executes destructive actions (deleting pages, removing members) without an explicit, separate confirmation prompt — even if the action plan includes them.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## How Jetie AI Stays in Context

The quality of Jetie AI's responses depends on how much relevant context it can see. At any moment, the AI reads from three layers:

| Context layer | What it includes |
|---|---|
| **Current page** | The full text and blocks of the page you have open right now. |
| **Linked pages** | Any pages referenced via `@page-name` links from the current page (one level deep). |
| **Task backlog** | Your open tasks across all databases in the current workspace, filtered to what is relevant to the query. |

You can explicitly expand the AI's context by opening additional pages before invoking Chat or Autopilot mode, or by using the **Add context** button in the AI panel to pin specific pages or databases to the session.

## Context Memory

Jetie AI remembers the projects and goals you have discussed across sessions, so you do not have to re-explain your situation every time you open a new conversation. Memory is scoped per workspace — the AI's understanding of your team's projects in one workspace does not bleed into another.

For a detailed breakdown of how context memory works, how long it persists, and how to clear or edit remembered information, see the [Context Memory guide](/ai/context-memory).

## Privacy and Data Security

<Note>
  **Your data is never used to train AI models.** All AI inference runs against an isolated, encrypted snapshot of your workspace content. Jetie does not share your pages, tasks, or documents with any third-party model provider for training purposes. Your workspace data belongs to you.
</Note>

Additional privacy commitments:

- AI context snapshots are encrypted at rest and in transit.
- Context snapshots are discarded at the end of each session unless you explicitly enable Context Memory.
- Workspace Admins can disable the AI assistant entirely under **Settings → AI**.
- Enterprise customers can opt into a private deployment where AI inference runs within their own cloud boundary.

## Limitations

Jetie AI is powerful, but it has boundaries worth knowing:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Approval required for edits" icon="shield-check">
    The AI cannot modify, create, or delete any content without your explicit approval. Every proposed change appears as a diff you must accept before it is applied.
  </Card>
  <Card title="No external browsing" icon="globe">
    Jetie AI works entirely within your workspace context. It cannot browse the web, access external APIs, or read files outside of Jetie.
  </Card>
  <Card title="No billing or settings changes" icon="lock">
    The AI cannot change workspace settings, manage members, or interact with billing — even in Autopilot mode.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Context window limits apply" icon="ruler">
    Very large workspaces may exceed the AI's context window. Use the **Add context** panel to focus the AI on the most relevant pages when working in large workspaces.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Usage Limits by Plan

| Plan | AI Requests per Month | Autopilot Actions per Month |
|---|---|---|
| **Free** | 50 | 10 |
| **Pro** | 500 | 100 |
| **Business** | Unlimited | Unlimited |

<Tip>
  An "AI request" counts as one Chat query or one Autopilot plan generation. Individual steps within an approved Autopilot plan do not consume additional requests — only the initial plan generation does.
</Tip>

<Note>
  Usage resets on the first day of each calendar month. Pro and Business plan subscribers can monitor current usage under **Settings → AI → Usage**.
</Note>
